About Me

I am a master's thesis student at the Georgia Institute of Technology in Aerospace Engineering, advised by Professor Yongxin Chen, working on state estimation and planning for mobile robotics. I am supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship (GRFP), and I plan to pursue a PhD.

Previously, I worked on hypersonic guidance and control with Professor John Dec related to the HyperSat program. I have also spent extensive time in the startup world, having interned in guidance, navigation, and control (GNC) at both Astranis and Mach Industries.

Research Interests

  • Computer Vision

    Primarily applied to 3D scene understanding, Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), and state estimation.

  • Trajectory Planning

    The intersection of vision, estimation and mapping for trajectory optimization.
